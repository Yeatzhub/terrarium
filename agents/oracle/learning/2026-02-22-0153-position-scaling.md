# Position Scaling: When to Add, When to Exit

**Oracle Quick Study**  
*Topic: Trading Concept (Risk & Sizing)*  
*Date: 2026-02-22 01:53 AM CT*

---

## TL;DR

Scaling works—but only in the right direction. Add to winners (pyramiding), never losers (averaging down). Scale out at predetermined levels, not emotions. Poor scaling destroys accounts; proper scaling compounds winners exponentially.

---

## The Two Scaling Methods

### 1. Scaling In (Pyramiding)

Adding to winning positions as they move in your favor.

**The Math:**
- Entry 1: 100 shares at $50
- Entry 2: 50 shares at $55 (add on strength)
- Entry 3: 25 shares at $60 (add on continued strength)
- Total: 175 shares, average cost $52.86

**Why it works:** Winners confirm thesis. Losers prove thesis wrong.

### 2. Scaling Out (Partial Exits)

Taking partial profits at predetermined levels.

**The Math:**
- Position: 100 shares at $50
- Exit 1: 33 shares at $55 (take 1R)
- Exit 2: 33 shares at $60 (take 2R)
- Exit 3: 34 shares at $65+ (trail or let run)

**Why it works:** Locks in profits while maintaining upside exposure.

## The Golden Rules of Scaling

### Rule 1: Never Scale Into Losers

**Averaging Down (What NOT to Do):**
- Buy 100 shares at $50
- Price drops to $45
- Buy 100 more at $45 (average now $47.50)
- Price drops to $40
- You now have 200 shares at $47.50, down $1,500

**Why it fails:** You're increasing position size as thesis proves wrong. One bad trade can blow the account.

### Rule 2: Only Scale Into Winners (Pyramiding)

**Proper Pyramiding:**
- Buy 100 shares at $50
- Price rises to $55, add 50 shares
- Price rises to $60, add 25 shares
- Stop at $53 (breakeven + cushion)

**Why it works:** Risk decreases as position grows. Winner confirms thesis.

### Rule 3: Reduce Size with Each Addition

**The Pyramid Structure:**

| Addition | Size | Cumulative | Risk Profile |
|----------|------|------------|--------------|
| Initial | 100% | 100% | Full risk |
| Add 1 | 50% | 150% | Risk at breakeven |
| Add 2 | 25% | 175% | Risk at +1R |
| Add 3 | 12.5% | 187.5% | Risk at +2R |

**Each addition is smaller than the last.** This ensures your average price stays closer to current price than your initial entry.

### Rule 4: Move Stops to Breakeven Before Adding

**The Safety Check:**
- Initial entry: $50, stop at $48 (2% risk)
- Price moves to $52
- Before adding, move stop to $50 (breakeven)
- Now add 50% size at $52
- New stop at $50 = zero risk on initial position

**Never add while still at risk on initial position.**

## Scaling In Framework

### When to Add

**Criteria Checklist:**
- [ ] Position is profitable (at least 1R)
- [ ] Stop moved to breakeven or better
- [ ] Trend structure intact (HH/HL for longs)
- [ ] Price at key level (breakout, pullback to support)
- [ ] Volume confirms (not adding into exhaustion)

**Add Points:**
1. **Breakout confirmation:** Price breaks resistance, add on close above
2. **Pullback to support:** Price retraces to moving average or prior resistance
3. **New structure formed:** Higher low confirmed, add above swing high

### How Much to Add

**Conservative:** 25-33% of initial size
**Moderate:** 50% of initial size
**Aggressive:** 75-100% of initial size (increases risk of whipsaw)

**Recommendation:** 50% additions, max 2-3 additions per position.

### Where to Place Stops After Adding

**Option 1: Breakeven on All**

After first add, move all stops to average entry.

**Pro:** Locks in profit
**Con:** Vulnerable to normal pullbacks

**Option 2: Tiered Stops**

- Initial position stop: Breakeven
- First addition stop: Just below entry
- Second addition stop: At first addition entry

**Pro:** Gives each tier breathing room
**Con:** More complex, partial exits more likely

**Option 3: Trail by ATR**

- All positions trail at 2 ATR from highest price
- Simple, adjusts to volatility
- **Best for most situations**

## Scaling Out Framework

### When to Scale Out

**At predetermined levels, not emotions.**

**Level-Based Exits:**

| Target | Size | Reason |
|--------|------|--------|
| 1R | 25-33% | Lock profit, reduce risk |
| 2R | 25-33% | Take meaningful profit |
| 3R+ | Trail remaining | Capture trend |

**Structure-Based Exits:**

| Signal | Size |
|--------|------|
| First target hit | 33% out |
| Reversal pattern | 50% out |
| Stop hit | Remaining out |

### The Breakeven Trigger

When price reaches 1R profit:
- Move stop to breakeven
- Option: Take 20-25% profit at 1R if conviction low

**This eliminates risk while maintaining upside.**

### The Full Exit vs. Scale Out Decision

**Full Exit (100% out) when:**
- Target is major resistance with low probability of extension
- Thesis is fully played out
- Risk event approaching (earnings, FOMC)
- Emotional trading becoming issue

**Scale Out when:**
- Strong trend with room to run
- Multiple targets at different significances
- Want to reduce stress while participating
- High conviction but uncertain magnitude

## The Mathematics of Pyramiding

### Example: 3-Add Pyramid

**Setup:**
- Account: $50,000
- Risk per trade: 1% = $500
- Entry: $100, Stop: $95 (5% risk)
- Initial size: $500 / $5 = 100 shares

**Pyramid Plan:**
| Action | Price | Shares | Avg Cost | Stop | Risk |
|--------|-------|--------|----------|------|------|
| Entry | $100 | 100 | $100 | $95 | $500 |
| Add 1 | $105 | 50 | $101.67 | $100 | $0 |
| Add 2 | $110 | 25 | $103.33 | $105 | -$42 |
| Trail | $115 | — | $103.33 | $107.50 | +$167 |

**Outcome A: Price goes to $120**
- Position: 175 shares
- Exit: $120
- Profit: ($120 - $103.33) × 175 = $2,917
- Risk taken: $500 initial → 5.8R profit

**Outcome B: Price reverses from $115 to $107.50 (stop)**
- Stopped out at $107.50
- Profit: ($107.50 - $103.33) × 175 = $730
- Profit despite reversal: +1.5R

**Key:** The profitable trade would have been $500 if held without pyramid. With pyramid, it's $2,917—or $730 even if stopped. Pyramiding amplifies winners.

## The Mathematics of Scaling Out

### Example: 3-Part Scale

**Setup:**
- Entry: $100
- Target 1: $105 (1R)
- Target 2: $110 (2R)
- Target 3: Trail (variable)
- Stop: $95
- Size: 150 shares

**Execution:**

| Level | Price | Shares | Profit | Remaining |
|-------|-------|--------|--------|-----------|
| Entry | $100 | — | $0 | 150 |
| T1 | $105 | 50 out | +$250 | 100 |
| T2 | $110 | 50 out | +$500 | 50 |
| Trail | $120 | 50 out | +$1,000 | 0 |

**Total profit:** $1,750 on 2R trade (with extension to 4R)

**Alternative: Full Exit at $110**
- 150 shares × $10 = $1,500

**Scaling out captured extra $250** while reducing risk early.

## When NOT to Scale

### Avoid Pyramiding When:
- Market is choppy/range-bound
- Volume declining on advance
- Approaching major resistance without clear break
- News event imminent
- Position already at max risk allocation (6% total open)
- First addition stopped out (trend failing)

### Avoid Scaling Out When:
- Trend is extremely strong with no resistance
- Position is small (scaling doesn't make sense)
- Risk event already passed
- Conviction is maximum

## Scaling by Market Type

| Market Condition | Scaling Strategy |
|------------------|------------------|
| **Strong trend** | Pyramid 2-3 times, trail remaining |
| **Weak trend** | Scale out at 1R and 2R, no pyramid |
| **Range** | No pyramid, full exit at target |
| **Volatile** | Smaller additions, wider stops |
| **Low volatility** | Can pyramid more aggressively |

## Common Scaling Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Adding to Losers
"Let me average down to get a better price."
**Result:** Larger loss when thesis is wrong.

### Mistake 2: Adding Too Much
"Add another 100% at $55."
**Result:** Average price too close to current. Whipsaw stops you out overall.

### Mistake 3: Adding Without Moving Stop
"Add at $55, keep stop at $48."
**Result:** Increased risk on confirmed winner. Break-even takes out larger position.

### Mistake 4: Scaling Out Too Early
"Take 50% at 0.5R, lock in profits."
**Result:** Missed the big move. Guaranteed suboptimal performance.

### Mistake 5: Not Planning Ahead
"I'll figure it out when I'm profitable."
**Result:** Emotional decisions, inconsistent execution.

**Solution:** Write your scaling rules BEFORE you enter. Execute mechanically.

## Quick Scaling Rules Card

**Scaling In (Pyramid):**
- Only on profitable positions
- Stop at breakeven before adding
- Each addition smaller than last (50% max)
- Add on breakouts or pullbacks to support
- Max 2-3 additions per position

**Scaling Out:**
- Take 25-33% at 1R (move stop to breakeven)
- Take 25-33% at 2R
- Trail remaining 33-50%
- If 3R+ move, consider holding for trend

**Never:**
- Add to a losing position
- Add without moving stop
- Scale without a plan
- Add more than initial position

## Summary

| Scaling Type | Purpose | Rule |
|--------------|---------|------|
| **Pyramid In** | Amplify winners | Smaller adds, stops protected |
| **Scale Out** | Lock profits | Predetermined levels, not emotions |
| **Averaging Down** | Amplify losers | NEVER DO THIS |

**Oracle's Note:** *Scaling is a double-edged sword. Done correctly, it transforms 2R trades into 5R winners. Done incorrectly, it turns 1R losses into 3R losses. The difference is direction: add to winners, never losers. The math is unambiguous—scaling into confirmed winners compounds your edge. Scaling into losers compounds your mistakes.*

**The Framework:** Plan your adds and exits before entry. Execute mechanically. Let winners grow while protecting capital. Scaling isn't about being right—it's about maximizing when you're right and minimizing when you're wrong.
