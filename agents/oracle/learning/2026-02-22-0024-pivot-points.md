# Pivot Points: Institutional Support/Resistance Levels

**Oracle Quick Study**  
*Topic: Indicator Use Case*  
*Date: 2026-02-22 12:24 AM CT*

---

## TL;DR

Pivot Points are calculated support/resistance levels derived from yesterday's High, Low, and Close. Institutional traders use them. They're objective, self-fulfilling, and reset daily. Use them for entries, targets, and risk management—especially in the first 2 hours of trading.

---

## The Calculation

**Classic Pivot Points:**

```
Pivot (P) = (High + Low + Close) / 3

Resistance Levels:
R1 = (2 × P) - Low
R2 = P + (High - Low)
R3 = High + 2 × (High - Low)

Support Levels:
S1 = (2 × P) - High
S2 = P - (High - Low)
S3 = Low - 2 × (High - Low)
```

**What this means:**
- Pivot = yesterday's average price (weighted)
- R1/S1 = primary trading range boundaries
- R2/S2 = extended range (2nd level)
- R3/S3 = extreme levels (rarely tested)

## Pivot Point Types

| Type | Calculation Basis | Best For |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| **Classic** | Previous day HLC | Standard intraday trading |
| **Fibonacci** | Classic + Fibonacci extensions | Forex, crypto |
| **Camarilla** | Different formula, more levels | Scalping, tight ranges |
| **Woodie** | Emphasizes Open price | Markets with significant gaps |
| **Demark** | Uses Open and conditional formulas | Trend identification |

**Recommendation:** Start with Classic pivots. They're the most widely used and respected.

## The Trading Day at Pivots

### Pre-Market (Before Open)

1. Calculate today's pivots from yesterday's HLC
2. Mark P, S1, S2, R1, R2 on chart
3. Note where price opens relative to Pivot:
   - **Above P:** Bullish bias
   - **Below P:** Bearish bias
   - **At P:** Neutral, wait for direction

### Opening Range (First 30 min)

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Opens above P, holds above | Long bias, target R1 |
| Opens below P, holds below | Short bias, target S1 |
| Opens at P, breaks up | Long, target R1 |
| Opens at P, breaks down | Short, target S1 |
| Opens above R1 | Gap up, watch for fade to R1/P |

### Midday (11 AM - 2 PM)

- Pivots act as support/resistance in range
- fades at S1/R1 common if morning was directional
- Mean reversion strategies between S1 and R1

### Power Hour (Last Hour)

- Price often moves toward Pivot (institutional benchmarking)
- If below P, bias toward P
- If above P, may extend or return to P

## The Five Core Strategies

### Strategy 1: Pivot Bounce (Most Reliable)

**Setup:**
1. Price approaches Pivot level
2. Slows down, shows rejection (candlestick pattern)
3. Enter on bounce

**Long Entry:**
- Price pulls back to P from above
- Hammer, engulfing, or rejection wick at P
- Entry on close of rejection candle
- Stop below P (or S1 for wider stop)
- Target: R1

**Win Rate:** 60-65% when confirmed with volume

### Strategy 2: Breakout at S1/R1

**Setup:**
1. Price consolidates at S1 or R1
2. Breaks through with momentum candle
3. Target next level

**Breakout Rules:**
- Wait for close beyond level (not just wick)
- Volume should be 1.5x average
- Target: S2 or R2

**Conservative Entry:**
- Wait for retest of broken level
- Enter on bounce off level (now support/resistance flipped)

### Strategy 3: Gap Fill to Pivot

**Setup:**
1. Price gaps open significantly above/below P
2. Gap > 0.5% (or significant for instrument)
3. Price action shows weakness at extreme

**The Trade:**
- Fade the gap toward Pivot
- Gap fills to P with 60-70% probability
- Stop beyond gap extreme
- Target: Pivot

**Warning:** Avoid fading gaps with major news catalyst.

### Strategy 4: Pivot Range Trading (S1-R1)

**Setup:**
1. Price opens between S1 and R1
2. Morning tests both levels without breakout
3. Range established

**The Trade:**
- Buy at S1 (or near P from below)
- Sell at R1 (or near P from above)
- Stop beyond range
- Target: opposite boundary or P

**Best for:** Midday, low-volatility days

### Strategy 5: Second Level Extension (S2/R2)

**Setup:**
1. Price breaks through S1 or R1
2. No immediate reversal
3. Momentum continues

**The Trade:**
- Enter on pullback to broken S1/R1 (now support/resistance)
- Target: S2 or R2
- Trail stop using P or S1/R1

**Note:** S2/R2 touches are less common (~15-20% of days). High conviction required.

## Pivot Point Quality Scoring

| Factor | Points | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Level convergence** | +3 | Pivot aligns with HTF S/R or moving average |
| **Time of day** | +2 | First 2 hours or power hour |
| **Opening bias** | +2 | Open confirms pivot direction |
| **Volume** | +1 | Above average at level |
| **First touch** | +1 | First test of level (fresh level) |

**Scoring:**
- 7-9 points: High probability trade
- 4-6 points: Standard trade
- 0-3 points: Skip

## Weekly and Monthly Pivots

### Weekly Pivots

- Calculated from last week's HLC
- More significant than daily pivots
- Act as major support/resistance

**Use:**
- Daily R1 aligning with Weekly P = very strong resistance
- Daily S1 aligning with Weekly S1 = confluence support

### Monthly Pivots

- Calculated from last month's HLC
- Major institutional levels
- Test rarely, but reaction is strong

**Use:**
- Monthly Pivot as major support/resistance
- Monthly S2/R2 as extreme levels (earnings, major news)

## Pivot + Confluence

### Pivot + Moving Average
- Pivot at 50 SMA = major level
- Pivot at 200 SMA = institutional level

### Pivot + Volume Profile
- Pivot at Value Area High/Low = institutional reference
- Pivot at POC (Point of Control) = highest acceptance

### Pivot + Previous High/Low
- R1 aligns with yesterday's high = double resistance
- S1 aligns with yesterday's low = double support

### Pivot + Opening Range
- OR high at R1 = extended target
- OR low at S1 = extended target

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Trading Every Level
Not all pivot touches are trades. Wait for confirmation.

### 2. Ignoring Opening Position
Open above P = bullish bias. Don't short every R1 touch in a bullish day.

### 3. Using Wrong Calculation
Different platforms use different formulas. Verify which pivots your platform uses.

### 4. Not Considering Gaps
Gap opens change the calculation for some pivot types (Woodie, Demark).

### 5. Blind Targets
R2 isn't always reached. Take profits at R1; don't hold through for R2 without conviction.
