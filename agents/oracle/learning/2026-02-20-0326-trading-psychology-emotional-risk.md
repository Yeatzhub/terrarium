## The Real Risk Isn't Market Risk

**90% of trading failure = Psychology failure.**

You can have perfect edge, flawless system, and still lose money because:
- Revenge trading after losses
- Overstaying winners (greed)
- Cutting winners short (fear)
- Trading outside your system (FOMO)
- Size escalation after wins (euphoria)

---

## The Emotional Cycle

### Winning Phase (DANGER)

| Stage | Behavior | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| Confidence | Normal, system working | Manageable |
| Euphoria | Increasing size, overtrading | **Account threat** |
| Invincibility | Ignoring stops, YOLO trades | **Blowup imminent** |
| Crash | Major loss, reality returns | Drawdown |

**Rule:** After 3 consecutive wins → **reduce size 25%** until normal mindset returns.

### Losing Phase (DANGER)

| Stage | Behavior | Risk |
|-------|----------|------|
| Concern | Normal, variance | Manageable |
| Frustration | Forcing trades | Edge erosion |
| Desperation | Revenge trading, size up | **Account threat** |
| Doom | All hope lost or crazy gambles | **Blowup** |

**Rule:** After 2 consecutive losses → **mandatory 30-minute break**. After 3 → **stop for day**.

## Psychological Risk Controls

### 1. Pre-Trading Routine

**15 minutes before market open:**
- [ ] Review trading plan (not P&L)
- [ ] Set daily loss limit (write it down)
- [ ] Define max trades for day
- [ ] Set position size caps
- [ ] Review: "I will follow my system"

**Why:** Sets cognitive framework before emotions kick in.

### 2. Physical State Check

| State | Can Trade? | Action |
|-------|------------|--------|
| Well rested | Yes | Proceed |
| Tired | No | Skip day |
| Hungry | No | Eat first |
| Stressed | No | Meditate/exercise |
| Rushed | No | Wait until calm |
| Excited | Caution | Reduce size 25% |
| Angry | **Never** | Day off |

**Your brain trades your body.** Trade in suboptimal state = suboptimal decisions.

### 3. Trading State Monitoring

**Every hour, self-assess (1-10 scale):**
- Calm vs. Anxious
- Focused vs. Scattered
- Patient vs. Impulsive
- Confident vs. Doubtful

**If any score drops below 6 → Take 15-minute break**

## Cognitive Biases in Trading

| Bias | Manifestation | Antidote |
|------|---------------|----------|
| **Confirmation** | Only see charts supporting your position | Force counter-argument |
| **Recency** | Last trade influences next | Reset mental slate each trade |
| **Anchoring** | Fixated on entry price | Focus on current structure |
| **Sunk Cost** | Can't exit loser "because I'm down" | Pre-defined stops only |
| **FOMO** | Chase moves after they started | Wait for pullback/retest |
| **Overconfidence** | Size up after wins | Fixed position sizing |
| **Loss Aversion** | Hold losers too long, cut winners | Automated exits, mechanical |
| **Gambler's Fallacy** | "Due for a win" after losses | Each trade independent |

## The System Is Your Psychologist

**Your trading system exists to remove emotion.**

### Non-Negotiables (Never Break)

1. **Stop losses** — Set before entry, never move against you
2. **Daily loss limit** — Hit it = stop trading, no exceptions
3. **Position sizing** — Fixed %, never size up/down emotionally
4. **Setup criteria** — A+ only, no "close enough"
5. **Trade journal** — Every trade logged with emotion note

### Mechanical Rules

- All entries = limit orders or alerts (no impulsive market orders)
- All exits = stops/targets pre-set (no manual clicking)
- Review = end of day only (not during session)
- No P&L checking between trades

---

## Post-Loss Protocol

**Immediately after a losing trade:**

1. **Close chart** for 5 minutes
2. **Deep breath** — 4 counts in, hold, out, hold
3. **Physical reset** — Stand up, stretch, walk
4. **Journal entry:**
   - Was setup A+? (Y/N)
   - Did I follow system? (Y/N)
   - Emotion (1-10)
   - What will I do different? (if anything)
5. **Only then** → next setup

**NEVER:**
- Immediately re-enter same symbol
- Size up "to make it back"
- Switch timeframes to find an entry
- Trade a different setup out of frustration

---

## Post-Win Protocol

**Euphoria kills more accounts than despair.**

1. **Acknowledge** but don't celebrate
2. **Review:** Was it system edge or luck?
3. **Size check:** Reduce 25% for next trade (emotional dampener)
4. **Journal:** Note confidence level (keep it humble)
5. **Reset:** Treat next trade as fresh, independent

## The "Not Trading" Edge

**Best trades you'll make = No trade at all**

When to skip:
- No A+ setups present
- Emotional state compromised
- Market conditions unfavorable (low vol, chop)
- Approaching daily/weekly limits
- Pre-news, pre-Fed, pre-earnings

**Skill:** Sitting on hands is a skill. Practice it.

## Mindfulness Techniques

### Pre-Trade Breath (10 seconds)
- Inhale 4 counts
- Hold 4 counts
- Exhale 4 counts
- Hold 4 counts
- Ask: "Is this an A+ setup?"

### The Pause Rule
Before every entry:
- Count to 5
- Re-read setup criteria
- Confirm stop placement
- Only then click

### End-of-Day Ritual
- Close all positions (or set overnight stops)
- Review journal, not P&L
- Note 1 thing done well
- Note 1 thing to improve
- Walk away. Market will be there tomorrow.

## Warning Signs You're Trading Emotionally

- [ ] Checking P&L more than charts
- [ ] Trading outside your system
- [ ] Sizing up after wins
- [ ] Revenge trading after losses
- [ ] Moving stops to avoid loss
- [ ] Entering without pre-defined exit
- [ ] Trading just to "be in the market"
- [ ] Feeling anxious/angry during trades
- [ ] Can't sleep thinking about positions
- [ ] Breaking rules "just this once"

**If 2+ checked → Stop trading immediately. Reset.**
