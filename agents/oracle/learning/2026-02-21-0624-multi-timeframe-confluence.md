## The Hierarchy Framework

| Timeframe | Purpose | Question It Answers |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| **Higher (HTF)** | Structure & Direction | "What is the trend? Where are key levels?" |
| **Middle (MTF)** | Setup & Confirmation | "Is price pulling back to value? Is there a pattern?" |
| **Lower (LTF)** | Execution & Entry | "Where exactly do I enter? Where is my invalidation?" |

**Example Configurations:**
- Swing trader: Weekly → Daily → 4H
- Day trader: Daily → 1H → 15M
- Scalper: 1H → 15M → 5M

## The Confluence Checklist

### Step 1: HTF Direction (The Filter)

Only trade in HTF direction. Period.

```
HTF_Bullish = Price > HTF_EMA(50) AND Price > HTF_EMA(200)
HTF_Bearish = Price < HTF_EMA(50) AND Price < HTF_EMA(200)
HTF_Neutral = Conflicting EMAs OR price between them

Rule: NO TRADE if HTF_Neutral. Exception: mean reversion specialist only.
```

**Key HTF Elements:**
- Trend direction (higher highs/lows OR lower highs/lows)
- Major support/resistance zones
- Untested liquidity levels (where will price likely go?)

### Step 2: MTF Setup (The Trigger Zone)

Look for these alignment patterns:

**Long Setup:**
- HTF trending up
- MTF pulling back to HTF support OR key moving average (20/50 EMA)
- MTF showing consolidation, falling wedge, or bullish reversal pattern

**Short Setup:**
- HTF trending down
- MTF rallying into HTF resistance OR key moving average
- MTF showing bearish reversal pattern

**MTF Confirmation Methods:**
1. Price at key level + candlestick reversal pattern
2. Price at key level + MTF RSI divergence
3. Price at key level + volume signature shift

### Step 3: LTF Entry Execution (The Precision)

Once MTF setup zone identified, drop to LTF for:

**Entry Techniques:**
- **Breakout:** LTF price breaks above resistance (long) / below support (short)
- **Pullback:** LTF shows micro-pullback to first support after confirming move
- **Rejection:** LTF shows clear rejection wick at key level MTF zone

**Invalidation:** Stop loss goes beyond HTF level (not LTF noise).

## The Confluence Score

Rate your setups. Higher score = higher conviction = larger size (within risk limits).

| Factor | +1 Point | Conditions |
|--------|----------|------------|
| Trend | HTF aligned with trade direction | EMAs stacked, clear structure |
| Level | At major HTF support/resistance | Tested 2+ times previously |
| Pattern | Clear reversal/continuation pattern | Wedges, H&S, double tops/bottoms |
| Momentum | MTF RSI/MACD divergence OR momentum shift | RSI below 30 rising (long) |
| Volume | Volume surge on reversal attempt | 150%+ of 20-period average |
| Correlation | Sector/index aligned | SPY +0.5% on long stock setup |

**Scoring:**
- 5-6 points: High conviction (full position size)
- 3-4 points: Medium conviction (half position)
- 0-2 points: Skip trade

## Common Multi-Timeframe Mistakes

1. **Analysis paralysis** — Don't check 5+ timeframes. 3 is enough.

2. **Timeframe confusion** — LTF entry doesn't override HTF invalidation. If HTF level breaks, you're wrong regardless of LTF beauty.

3. **Mismatching volatility** — An M15 setup during NFP on a daily chart = noise. Know your session volatility.

4. **Ignoring structure** — Trading LTF breakout against HTF trend. You will become exit liquidity.

## Quick Implementation Workflow

```
1. Open HTF: Mark trend, key levels, liquidity targets
2. Open MTF: Wait for price to reach HTF zone
3. Check MTF: Pattern forming? Momentum shifting?
4. Drop to LTF: Find specific entry, set stop beyond HTF level
5. Calculate R:R — minimum 1:2 against HTF target
6. Execute or pass
```
