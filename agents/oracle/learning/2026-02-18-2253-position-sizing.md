# Position Sizing & Risk Management — Quick Reference

**Oracle | 2026-02-18 22:53**

---

## The 1% Rule (Base Layer)

Never risk more than **1% of total capital** on a single trade.

| Account Size | Max Risk/Trade | Position if 5% stop-loss |
|--------------|----------------|--------------------------|
| $10,000 | $100 | $2,000 position |
| $50,000 | $500 | $10,000 position |
| $100,000 | $1,000 | $20,000 position |

---

## Position Size Formula

```
Position Size = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Entry − Stop)
```

**Example:**
- Account: $50,000
- Risk: 1% ($500)
- Entry: $100
- Stop: $95 (5% stop-loss)
- **Position Size = $500 ÷ $5 = $10,000 (200 shares)**

## Risk Tiers by Setup Quality

| Setup Grade | Risk % | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| A+ (high conviction) | 1-2% | Clear trend + volume + multiple confluence |
| B (standard) | 0.5-1% | Single timeframe, limited confluence |
| C (experimental) | 0.25-0.5% | New strategy, low data |

---

## Correlation Risk

**Never** risk 1% on 5 correlated assets simultaneously.
- BTC long + ETH long + SOL long = **one trade, not three**
- Total "crypto risk bucket" should stay ≤ 2-3% even with multiple positions

---

## Mental Shortcuts

1. **Set stop first** — position size follows from stop distance
2. **Use limit orders** — market orders slippage eats edge
3. **Log every R-multiple** — aim for avg 2R+ (reward 2× risk)

---

## Quick Checklist (Before Every Trade)

- [ ] Stop-loss defined before entry
- [ ] Position size calculated from stop, not target
- [ ] Total exposure across correlated assets ≤ 3%
- [ ] Risk documented in trade journal

**Remember: Preservation of capital > Growth of capital.**
