# Position Sizing: The Risk Control Most Traders Skip

**Date:** 2026-02-18  
**Topic:** Risk Management / Position Sizing  
**Time:** 15 min read

---

## The Core Problem

Most traders focus on *where* to enter (setups, indicators) but neglect *how much* to risk. A 50% win rate with poor sizing will bankrupt you faster than a 40% win rate with proper sizing.

## The 1% Rule (Baseline)

**Never risk more than 1% of total capital on a single trade.**

Example:
- Account: $50,000
- Max risk per trade: $500 (1% of $50k)
- Stop loss: 5% from entry
- Position size = $500 ÷ 0.05 = **$10,000 position** (20% of account, but only 1% at risk)

## Quick Sizing Formula

```
Position Size = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Entry - Stop Loss)
```

Or in shares:
```
Shares = Risk $ ÷ Risk Per Share
```

## Kelly Criterion (Advanced)

For optimal growth when you know your edge:
```
f* = (p × b - q) ÷ b

Where:
p = win probability
q = loss probability (1-p)
b = win/loss ratio
```

**Conservative approach:** Use "Half Kelly" or "Quarter Kelly" to reduce volatility.

## Practical Mental Models

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Wider stop needed | Reduce position size, not widen stop |
| High conviction | Still cap at 2-3% max (never "size up" on feelings) |
| Losing streak | Reduce risk to 0.5% until edge returns |
| Correlated positions | Sum the risk (3 positions at 1% each = 3% total risk) |

## The R-Multiple Concept

Measure trades by **R** = initial risk amount.

- 1R = breakeven (hit stop)
- 2R = 2x risk reward
- 3R+ = target for positive expectancy

**Key insight:** A system with 40% win rate and 2R average profit is profitable. A 60% win rate with 0.8R average is a slow bleed.

## Common Sizing Mistakes

1. **Fixed shares** (100 shares regardless of price/stop)
2. **Dollar-cost entry** ("I have $5k, buy whatever that gets me")
3. **Martingale** (doubling down on losers)
4. **Overexposure** (5+ correlated positions at 2% each = 10% risk)

## Bottom Line

Position sizing is the only part of trading you can control 100%. You can't control price action. You CAN control how much you lose when wrong. Master this before optimizing entry signals.
