# Position Sizing & Risk Management — Quick Reference

**Date:** 2026-02-18  
**Topic:** Risk Management Core Concept  
**Time:** 15 min read

---

## The Golden Rule

**Never risk more than 1-2% of account equity on a single trade.**

This isn't conservative — it's survival math. A string of 10 losing trades at 2% risk leaves you down 18.3%. At 10% risk per trade, you're down 65%.

---

## The Position Sizing Formula

```
Position Size = (Account Risk $) ÷ (Entry - Stop Loss)

Where:
- Account Risk $ = Account Balance × Risk %
- (Entry - Stop Loss) = Risk per share/unit
```

### Example
- Account: $50,000
- Risk: 1% = $500
- Entry: $100
- Stop: $95 (5% stop)
- Position: $500 ÷ $5 = **100 shares** ($10,000 position = 20% of account)

Notice: You're only risking $500, but the position is $10,000. Leverage is not risk.

---

## Practical Rules

| Rule | Why It Works |
|------|--------------|
| Hard stops at entry | Mental stops fail under stress |
| Risk adjusts for volatility | Wider stops = smaller size |
| Never add to losers | Averaging down is account suicide |
| Daily/weekly loss limits | Stop trading after -3% day, -6% week |
| Risk-Reward minimum 1:2 | Need $200 potential gain for $100 risk |

## Risk of Ruin Table

| Win Rate | Risk/Trade | Consecutive Losses to Ruin (50% drawdown) |
|----------|------------|------------------------------------------|
| 50% | 2% | ~35 trades |
| 50% | 5% | ~14 trades |
| 50% | 10% | ~7 trades |
| 40% | 5% | ~9 trades |

**Key insight:** Even a 60% win rate system goes bust with 10% risk per trade eventually.

---

## Quick Assessment Checklist

Before every trade, answer:
1. What's my stop? (Price level, not %)
2. What's my risk in dollars? (Account × 0.01 or 0.02)
3. Position size = Risk $ ÷ (Entry - Stop)
4. Is reward at least 2× risk?
5. What closes this trade? (Target hit, stop hit, time exit)

---

## Mental Hack

Size so that a **stop-out feels disappointing, not painful**. If you're emotional about the loss, you're trading too big.
