# Order Blocks: Smart Money's Footprint in Market Structure

**Oracle Quick Study**

---

## TL;DR

Order Blocks (OB) are the last opposing candle before a strong impulsive move—institutional accumulation/distribution zones that act as magnets for future price. Price returns to these zones ~70% of the time before continuing the original move.

---

## What Order Blocks Actually Are

An Order Block is not just any support/resistance—it's the **final candle/candles in a consolidation before aggressive price movement**. 

**The Logic:**
- Smart money cannot enter large positions at once (market impact)
- They accumulate over time (bearish OB before rally) or distribute (bullish OB before drop)
- The "last bearish candle before bullish impulse" = final distribution/selling absorption
- Price returns here to collect remaining liquidity before continuing

**Visual Signature:**
- Bullish OB: Last bearish candle(s) before strong bullish impulse
- Bearish OB: Last bullish candle(s) before strong bearish impulse

---

## Identification Rules

### Bullish Order Block Requirements

1. **Preceding Move:** Downtrend or consolidation
2. **The OB:** Bearish candle(s) immediately before bullish impulse
3. **Impulse Criteria:** 
   - Next 1-3 candles rally significantly (greater than recent ATR)
   - Close well above OB high
   - Volume spike (optional but confirms)
4. **Unmitigated:** Price has not yet returned to the OB

### Bearish Order Block Requirements

1. **Preceding Move:** Uptrend or consolidation
2. **The OB:** Bullish candle(s) immediately before bearish impulse
3. **Impulse Criteria:**
   - Next 1-3 candles drop significantly
   - Close well below OB low
   - Volume spike (optional)
4. **Unmitigated:** Price has not yet returned

### Invalid OBs (Skip These)

- OB already fully tested (mitigated)
- OB in middle of range without clear structure
- OB formed during low volatility/no clear impulse
- OB too old (>50 candles, strength degrades)

## The Order Block Hierarchy

Not all OBs are equal. Rank them:

| Rank | Criteria | Win Rate | Use |
|------|----------|----------|-----|
| **S-tier** | Weekly/Daily OB + HTF structure + volume spike | ~75% | Major swing entries |
| **A-tier** | H4 OB at HTF key level + clean impulse | ~65% | Swing entries |
| **B-tier** | H1 OB with M15 confirmation | ~55% | Day trades |
| **C-tier** | M5 OB, no higher confluence | ~45% | Scalps only, tight stops |

**Rule:** Only trade A-tier and above unless you're scalping with tight risk management.

## The Entry Framework

### Long Setup (Bullish OB)

**Context:**
- HTF trend bullish OR range with clear structure
- Price approaching unmitigated bullish OB

**Entry Methods:**

1. **Limit at OB** (Aggressive)
   - Entry: Limit at OB high
   - Stop: Below OB low (10-20 pips buffer)
   - Target: Next HTF resistance / 1:2 minimum

2. **Confirmation Entry** (Conservative)
   - Wait for price to enter OB zone
   - Look for: rejection wick, bullish engulfing, or Stoch/MACD reversal
   - Enter on close of confirmation candle
   - Stop: Below OB low or below rejection wick

3. **Breaker Entry** (Advanced)
   - OB gets broken (low taken out)
   - Wait for price to reclaim OB
   - Entry on reclaim
   - This becomes a "breaker block"—often stronger than original OB

### Short Setup (Bearish OB)

Mirror the above:
- Entry at OB low or confirmation
- Stop above OB high
- Target next support / 1:2 minimum

## Risk Management: The OB Edge

**Why OBs improve R:R:**

Your stop goes **below the OB low** (longs), which is where the smart money entered. If price breaks that, the thesis is wrong immediately—not 50 pips later.

**Examples:**

| Setup | Entry | Stop | Target | R:R |
|-------|-------|------|--------|-----|
| OB Limit | $100.50 | $100.00 (OB low) | $102.00 | 1:3 |
| Standard Support | $100.50 | $99.50 | $102.00 | 1:1.5 |

**Same move, double the reward-to-risk.**

## Order Block + Confluence

Stack these for higher probability:

1. **Fair Value Gap (FVG) inside OB** — Price tends to fill FVG before moving
2. **Previous Session High/Low** — OB aligned with session liquidity
3. **Fibonacci retracement** — OB at 0.5 or 0.618 level
4. **Volume Imbalance** — Volume profile shows volume gap at OB zone
5. **Killzone timing** — OB hit during London/NY overlap

**The Setup:**
- Daily bullish OB
- At 0.5 fib retracement of last swing
- Near previous day high (liquidity sweep target)
- M15 shows rejection candle
- Enter long

## Common Mistakes

1. **Trading mitigated OBs** — Once price returns and reacts, the edge is gone. Look for fresh OBs.

2. **Using M5/M1 OBs without HTF** — Low timeframe OBs fail constantly. Always check HTF alignment.

3. **Entering before price reaches OB** — "Close enough" costs you. Wait for price to hit the zone.

4. **Ignoring the trend** — Bearish OB in strong uptrend = liquidity sweep before higher prices.

5. **Too-wide stops** — If you need 50+ pip stop on an OB, the OB is too large or wrong. Find better structure.
