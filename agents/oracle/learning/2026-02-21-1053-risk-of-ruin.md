# Risk of Ruin: The Math That Saves Accounts

**Oracle Quick Study**  
*Topic: Trading Concept (Risk & Sizing)*  
*Date: 2026-02-21 10:53 AM CT*

---

## TL;DR

Risk of Ruin (RoR) is the probability of losing enough of your account that you cannot continue trading. Even with a positive expectancy system, bad position sizing can guarantee ruin. Know your number.

---

## The Core Formula

```
Risk of Ruin = ((1 - Edge) / (1 + Edge)) ^ Capital_Units

Where:
- Edge = Win% - Loss% (simplified; use expectancy for accuracy)
- Capital_Units = Account / (Max_Drawdown_Level × Risk_Per_Trade)
```

**Simplified Reality Check:**

| Win Rate | Risk/Trade | RoR After 100 Trades | Verdict |
|----------|------------|---------------------|---------|
| 50% | 2% | ~5% | Safe |
| 50% | 5% | ~35% | Risky |
| 50% | 10% | ~85% | Gambling |
| 40% | 5% | ~65% | Dangerous |
| 60% | 10% | ~25% | Deceptively safe |

---

## The Kelly Criterion Connection

Kelly = (Win% / Loss% - Loss% / Win%) × Edge

**Your Kelly% tells you optimal risk per trade.** Most traders use 1/4 to 1/2 Kelly to smooth equity curves.

**Example:**
- System: 55% win rate, 1.5:1 average R:R
- Full Kelly: ~8.3% per trade
- Half Kelly (safer): ~4.15% per trade
- Quarter Kelly (institutional): ~2% per trade

**RoR at 2% risk with this system:** <1% over 500 trades
**RoR at 8% risk:** ~15% over 500 trades

## The Drawdown Multiplier Effect

Most traders calculate RoR on *initial* account size. Wrong. You must calculate on *depleted* account after drawdowns.

**The Sequence Problem:**

| Trade | Result | Account | After Drawdown |
|-------|--------|---------|----------------|
| 1-5 | 5 losses | $10,000 → $9,000 | -10% |
| 6 | Win +2R | $9,180 | Still -8.2% |
| Risk at $9k | 2% = $180 | Same %, smaller $ | |

**Key insight:** Fixed percentage sizing automatically adjusts RoR downward as account shrinks—but your emotional RoR increases (you're now risk-averse at the worst time).

## Fixed Fraction vs Fixed Ratio

### Fixed Fraction (Standard % Risk)
- Risk 1-2% of *current* account per trade
- RoR decreases as account shrinks
- Recovery requires larger absolute gains
- **Best for:** Most traders; simple to implement

### Fixed Ratio (Ryan Jones Method)
- Increase size only after X profit units
- Example: Start 1 lot, add 1 lot per $5,000 profit
- RoR remains constant regardless of account level
- **Best for:** Systems with consistent expectancy

**RoR Comparison at 50% win, 1:1 R:R:**

| Method | Sizing | RoR (50 trades) | Recovery Needed |
|--------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Fixed $ | $200/trade | 25% | Flat |
| Fixed % | 2%/trade | 4% | Percentage-based |
| Fixed Ratio | Steps at $5k | 8% | Step-based |

## Practical RoR Management

### Tier 1: Safety (RoR < 5%)
- Risk: 0.5-1% per trade
- Win rate: >50% or better R:R
- Max concurrent risk: 3-5%
- Account: Can withstand 10+ loss streak

**This is institutional/prop firm standard.**

### Tier 2: Moderate (RoR 5-20%)
- Risk: 1-2% per trade
- Win rate: >45%
- Max concurrent: 5-8%
- Account: Survives 6-8 loss streak

**Most successful retail traders.**

### Tier 3: Aggressive (RoR 20-50%)
- Risk: 2-5% per trade
- Win rate: Must be >55% or R:R > 2:1
- Max concurrent: 10%
- Account: Fragile; 1 bad month can end career

**Experienced traders only, with strict rules.**

### Tier 4: Gambling (RoR > 50%)
- Risk: >5% per trade
- Outcome: Mathematical certainty of ruin

**Not trading—entertainment with heavy cost.**

## The Serial Correlation Problem

Real markets aren't coin flips. Losses cluster.

**Monte Carlo Simulation Reality:**

50% win rate, 1:1 R:R, 2% risk:
- Independent events RoR: ~4%
- 10% serial correlation (losses cluster): ~12%
- 20% serial correlation (regime changes): ~28%

**Action:** Increase capital units by 50% in your RoR calculation to account for clustering.

## Calculate Your Personal RoR

1. **Find your stats:**
   - Total trades: N
   - Wins: W
   - Average win: $X
   - Average loss: $Y

2. **Calculate:**
   - Win rate: W/N
   - Average R:R: X/Y
   - Expectancy: (Win% × X) - (Loss% × Y)

3. **Simulate:**
   - Risk 1%: Run 1000-trade simulation, count blowups
   - Risk 2%: Same
   - Find your comfort threshold (<5% RoR ideal)

**Tools:** Use Kinfo, Edgewonk, or Python to backtest position sizing.

## Ruin Isn't Just Zero

**Practical Ruin Levels:**
- **Full ruin:** Account = $0 (rare, most quit first)
- **Psychological ruin:** Account down 50%, cannot trade objectively
- **Practical ruin:** Account too small to generate meaningful income

**Most traders hit psychological ruin at 30-40% drawdown.** Size so your 20% drawdown is "normal," not panic-inducing.

**At 50% drawdown, you must double your remaining account.** Most traders cannot do this psychologically.

## RoR Rules to Live By

1. **Never risk >2% per trade** unless you're betting with house money (profits)

2. **Never risk >6% total open** — correlation means your "diversified" trades move together

3. **Reduce size after 3 consecutive losses** — serial correlation is real

4. **Calculate RoR monthly** — your edge degrades, markets change

5. **Have a circuit breaker** — halt trading after 10% monthly drawdown

## Quick RoR Reference

Win Rate 50%, 1:1 R:R:
- 1% risk ≈ 1% RoR (500 trades)
- 2% risk ≈ 4% RoR
- 3% risk ≈ 15% RoR
- 5% risk ≈ 60% RoR

Win Rate 40%, 1.5:1 R:R (positive expectancy):
- 1% risk ≈ 3% RoR
- 2% risk ≈ 18% RoR
- 3% risk ≈ 55% RoR

**Lower win rate = size down aggressively.**
