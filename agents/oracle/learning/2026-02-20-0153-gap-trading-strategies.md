## What Are Gaps?

**Gap** = Price opens significantly above/below prior close with no trading in between.

**Types:**
- **Common Gap:** No catalyst, fills quickly
- **Breakaway Gap:** Starts new trend, high volume
- **Runaway Gap:** Mid-trend continuation
- **Exhaustion Gap:** End of trend, reverses

---

## Gap Statistics

| Gap Type | Fill Rate | Avg Time to Fill | Strategy |
|----------|-----------|------------------|----------|
| Small (<0.5%) | 75%+ | First hour | Fade (mean reversion) |
| Medium (0.5–1.5%) | 60% | Same day or next | Context dependent |
| Large (>1.5%) | 40% | Often partial | Trend continuation |
| Earnings gap | 35% | Rarely full | Trade catalyst direction |

**Key Insight:** Small gaps fill. Large gaps with catalyst trend. Size matters.

---

## Strategy 1: Gap Fade (Mean Reversion)

### When to Fade
- Gap < 0.8% with no major catalyst
- Into prior resistance (up gap) or support (down gap)
- Low relative volume
- Early in session (first 30 min)

### Setup
**Up Gap Fade:**
1. Gap up < 0.8%, no news
2. Price approaches prior resistance or 78.6% Fib
3. First 15-min candle shows exhaustion (long wick, low close)
4. Entry: Break of 15-min low
5. Stop: Above 15-min high
6. Target: Gap fill (prior close) or VWAP

**Down Gap Fade:** Reverse logic

**Risk Management:**
- Only attempt 1–2 gap fades per day
- If gap extends past 1% against position = stop
- Time stop: Exit if not working by 11:00 AM

## Strategy 2: Gap Continuation (Trend)

### When to Follow
- Gap > 1% with catalyst (earnings, news, upgrade)
- Breaks major resistance (up) or support (down)
- High relative volume (>150% average)
- Aligns with HTF trend

### Setup
**Breakaway Gap Long:**
1. Gap up >1.5% on earnings/news
2. Breaks 20-day high or key resistance
3. Volume 2× average
4. Wait for 5–15 min opening range
5. Entry: Break of opening range high
6. Stop: Below opening range low or VWAP
7. Target: 1.618 Fib extension or next resistance

**Success Factors:**
- Catalyst must be significant (beat/raise, M&A, FDA)
- Sector sympathy helps ( peers gapping same direction)
- Float <100M = more volatile, wider stops

---

## Strategy 3: Gap Retest (Best Risk/Reward)

**The Highest Probability Gap Play**

### Concept
Gap breaks level → pulls back to retest broken level → continues

### Setup
**Bullish Gap Retest:**
1. Gap up breaks clear resistance
2. First 30–60 min: Price pulls back to broken resistance
3. Broken resistance now acts as support
4. Entry: Hold of retest + bullish candle
5. Stop: Below retest low
6. Target: 2:1 minimum, often much more

**Example:**
- Stock gaps up through $100 resistance to $102
- Pulls back to $100.20 in first hour
- Holds $100, forms hammer candle
- Entry: $100.50, Stop: $99.80, Target: $104+

**Why it works:**
- Bears trapped shorting the gap
- Bulls defend broken resistance as support
- Volume dries up on retest (selling exhausted)
- Continuation pattern

---

## Strategy 4: Partial Gap Fill (Partial Fade)

**For Large Gaps with Catalyst**

### Concept
Large gaps rarely fill completely same day. Partial fill = re-entry for trend.

### Setup
**Bullish Partial Fill:**
1. Gap up >2% on catalyst
2. Fills 38.2–50% of gap in first hour
3. Finds support at:
   - Prior day high (now support)
   - 38.2% Fib of gap
   - VWAP
4. Entry: Reclaim of fill area with volume
5. Stop: Below fill low
6. Target: Extension of original move

**Statistics:**
- Large gaps fill 38–50% 70% of the time before continuing
- Full gap fill only 30% with strong catalyst

## Gap Trading Rules

### Pre-Market Analysis (Must Do)

1. **Why did it gap?**
   - Earnings: Check EPS, revenue, guidance
   - News: Material? Sector-wide?
   - Upgrade/Downgrade: Analyst firm credibility
   - Macro: Market-wide gap (SPY futures)

2. **Gap size vs. ATR:**
   - Gap < 0.5× ATR = likely fade
   - Gap > 1.5× ATR = likely trend
   - Gap > 3× ATR = extreme, watch for exhaustion

3. **Key levels:**
   - Mark prior day high/low
   - Mark 20-day high/low
   - Draw Fib of gap (0% = prior close, 100% = open)
   - Note VWAP from prior day

### Entry Timing

| Time | Strategy | Best For |
|------|----------|----------|
| 9:30–9:45 | Opening range break | Large gap continuation |
| 9:45–10:30 | Retest entries | Best R/R gap plays |
| 10:30–11:00 | Partial fill entries | Large gap, partial fade |
| 11:00+ | Avoid new gap trades | Momentum gone |

## Risk Management

### Hard Rules

1. **Never average into a fading gap** — if wrong, stop
2. **Gap >3% on no catalyst** = avoid (manipulation possible)
3. **Two gap trades max per day** — lower probability setups
4. **Size reduction:**
   - Fade: 0.75% risk (lower conviction)
   - Continuation: 1% risk
   - Retest: 1% risk (highest conviction)

### Stop Placement

| Strategy | Stop Location | Reason |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Fade | Above/below 15-min extreme | Invalidated if exceeded |
| Continuation | Below opening range or VWAP | Trend broken |
| Retest | Below retest low | Support failed |
| Partial fill | Below 61.8% of gap | Fill extending |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Fading every gap | Some gaps trend hard | Only fade small/no catalyst |
| Ignoring volume | Low volume gaps = traps | Require 1.5×+ for continuation |
| No catalyst check | Blind fades dangerous | Always know why it gapped |
| Chasing gaps after 10 AM | Best entries gone | Set alerts, skip if missed |
| Wrong gap size read | Large gaps rarely fill | Use ATR comparison |

## Gap Psychology

**Gap Up Psychology:**
- 9:30: Bulls euphoric, bears panicking
- 9:45: Smart money takes profits (often the peak)
- 10:00: Reality check — does it hold?
- 10:30: Direction confirmed (trend or fade)

**Gap Down Psychology:**
- 9:30: Fear peak, forced selling
- 9:45: Value buyers probe
- 10:00: Capitulation or bounce
- 10:30: True direction emerges

## Quick Reference: Gap Play Decision Tree

```
Gap Size?
├── < 0.5% → Fade candidate (check catalyst)
│   └── No catalyst? → Fade setup
│   └── Catalyst? → Skip or small continuation
├── 0.5–1.5% → Context dependent
│   └── HTF trend aligned? → Continuation
│   └── Into resistance? → Fade
├── > 1.5% → Trend likely
│   └── Strong catalyst? → Retest entry
│   └── Weak/no catalyst? → Partial fade
└── > 3% → Extreme (avoid or tiny size)
```
