# Position Sizing: The Risk Management Foundation

**Date:** 2026-02-18 04:53 AM CT  
**Topic:** Trading Risk Management - Position Sizing  
**Time Invested:** ~15 min

---

## Core Concept

Position sizing determines **how much you risk per trade**. It's the single most controllable factor in trading profitability—even a mediocre strategy can survive with proper sizing, while a great strategy will fail with poor sizing.

---

## The 1-2% Rule (Practical Baseline)

**Never risk more than 1-2% of total account equity on a single trade.**

| Account Size | 1% Risk | 2% Risk |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| $10,000      | $100    | $200    |
| $50,000      | $500    | $1,000  |
| $100,000     | $1,000  | $2,000  |

---

## Position Size Formula

```
Position Size = (Account Risk $) ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Loss Price)
```

### Example Calculation
- Account: $50,000
- Risk tolerance: 1% = $500
- Stock entry: $100
- Stop loss: $95 (5% stop)
- Position size: $500 ÷ $5 = **100 shares**
- Capital deployed: 100 × $100 = **$10,000 (20% of account)**

---

## Key Insights

1. **Risk ≠ Position Size** — A $20,000 position with 1% stop = $200 risk (0.4% of $50k account)
2. **Volatility adjustment** — Wider stops require smaller share counts to maintain same risk
3. **Correlation matters** — Multiple positions in same sector = concentrated risk even if each is 1%
4. **Compounding protection** — 10 consecutive 2% losses = ~18% drawdown (survivable)
   - Same trades at 5% risk = ~40% drawdown (career threatening)

---

## Practical Tactics

- **Tier your sizing**: 2% for A+ setups, 1% for B setups, 0.5% for experimental
- **Reduce size in chop**: When win rate drops below 50%, cut size by 50%
- **Volatility sizing**: Use ATR to normalize position sizes across different volatility regimes
- **Never override the math**: Emotion says "I'm confident, size up" — the numbers say no

---

## Quick Check

Before entering any trade, ask:
1. What's my stop price?
2. What's my risk amount (1-2% of account)?
3. How many shares/units achieves that exact risk?
4. Am I comfortable losing this amount if I'm wrong?

If the answer to #4 is no, **size down** until it is.

---

## Summary

Position sizing is your insurance policy against yourself. The market decides wins and losses—you only control how much you lose when wrong. Master sizing before mastering entries.
