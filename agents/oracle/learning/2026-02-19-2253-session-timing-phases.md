## Market Phases (US Equities)

Markets aren't random — they follow predictable behavioral patterns throughout the session. Trade the right phase with the right strategy.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Market (4:00–9:30 AM ET)

**Characteristics:**
- Low liquidity, wide spreads
- Driven by overnight news, earnings, economic data
- Futures/European markets influence direction

**Strategy:**
- **Watch, don't trade** (for most)
- Mark key levels from prior day
- Identify gap direction and size
- Set alerts for opening levels
- Plan trades, don't execute

**Exception:** Experienced traders can scalp high-volume pre-market movers with tight stops.

---

## Phase 2: Opening Range (9:30–10:30 AM ET)

**Characteristics:**
- Highest volatility of the day
- True range often established in first 30 min
- Institutional orders execute
- Amateur hour — retail FOMO peaks

**Strategies:**

### Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
1. Mark first 5-min or 15-min high/low
2. Wait for break with volume
3. Enter on retest of broken level
4. Stop: False break back into range
5. Target: 1.5–2× range extension

### Opening Reversal
1. Identify overnight gap
2. If gap >1.5% with no major catalyst = fade likely
3. Wait for 15–30 min exhaustion
4. Entry: Reversal candle at VWAP or extreme
5. Target: Gap fill or VWAP

**Risk Management:**
- **Phase rule:** Max 30% of daily risk in first hour
- High volatility = wider stops = smaller size
- Two losses in first hour = **stop for 30 min**

---

## Phase 3: Mid-Morning (10:30 AM–12:00 PM ET)

**Characteristics:**
- Volatility declines
- Trend establishes or range develops
- First real structure forms
- Best setup identification time

**Strategies:**

### Trend Continuation
- Opening trend continues but slows
- Pullbacks to VWAP or 9 EMA
- Entries on reclaim of micro-structure
- Higher win rate than opening

### Range Establishment
- If no clear trend, range forms
- Mark morning high/low
- Fade extremes, target VWAP
- Tighter stops than morning

**Timing Edge:** This is when A+ setups develop. Opening noise settles, direction clarifies.

---

## Phase 4: Lunch (12:00–2:00 PM ET)

**Characteristics:**
- Lowest volume of the day
- Spreads widen slightly
- Chop, whipsaws, false breaks
- Algos dominate

**The Rule:** **Don't trade lunch.**

**Exceptions:**
- Breakouts with 2× relative volume
- News-driven moves (Fed, earnings)
- Range extremes with clear S/R
- Options expiration (quarterly)

**If You Must:**
- Reduce size 50%
- Focus on mean reversion (fading extremes)
- Tight targets (1:1 RRR)
- Be flat by 1:30 PM

---

## Phase 5: Afternoon Session (2:00–3:30 PM ET)

**Characteristics:**
- Volume returns
- Trend resumption or reversal
- Institutional positioning for close
- Momentum builds

**Strategies:**

### Trend Resumption
- Morning trend continues
- Break of lunch range
- Entry: Break + retest
- Target: Prior day high/low or 2:1

### Afternoon Reversal
- Lunch range breakout fails
- Reversal pattern at key level
- Often catches retail on wrong side
- Quick moves, stay nimble

### VWAP Reversion
- Price returns to VWAP from lunch extreme
- VWAP magnet effect strong
- Scalp to VWAP, tight stop

---

## Phase 6: Power Hour (3:30–4:00 PM ET)

**Characteristics:**
- Second highest volume
- Momentum accelerates
- EOD positioning, options expiry
- Fade or trend — no middle ground

**Strategies:**

### Momentum Continuation
- Strong trend day = acceleration
- Break of afternoon structure
- Position for gap next day
- Hold through close (or partial)

### EOD Fade
- Extended from VWAP >2% no catalyst
- Afternoon climax pattern
- Quick mean reversion to VWAP
- Tight stops, must be decisive

### Options Pin
- Friday expiration
- Watch strikes with high gamma
- Price gravitates to max pain
- Don't fight the pin

**Risk:** Moves can reverse fast. Tight stops mandatory. Don't enter after 3:45 PM.

## Time-Based Adjustments

### Monday
- Often gap continuation from weekend news
- Slower to establish trend
- Wait longer for clarity

### Friday
- Light volume in morning
- Position squaring afternoon
- Watch options expiry (quarterly = high vol)

### Month-End/Quarter-End
- Institutional rebalancing
- Unusual flows
- Be cautious, reduce size

### Earnings Weeks
- High individual stock vol
- Index choppy
- Stock-specific > index trading
