## What is Volume Profile?

Histogram showing traded volume at each price level over a period. Unlike time-based charts, it reveals **price acceptance** — where buyers and sellers agreed.

**Key Levels:**
- **POC (Point of Control):** Most volume traded = strongest magnet
- **VAH (Value Area High):** Top 70% volume boundary = resistance
- **VAL (Value Area Low):** Bottom 70% volume boundary = support
- **HVN (High Volume Node):** Heavy volume = balance, hard to break
- **LVN (Low Volume Node):** Light volume = imbalance, quick moves through

---

## Core Use Cases

### 1. POC as Magnet/Pivot

**The Rule:** Price returns to POC 80%+ of the time. It's fair value.

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Price above POC | POC acts as support |
| Price below POC | POC acts as resistance |
| Price at POC | Decision point — break or hold |
| Extended from POC | Mean reversion likely |

**Trade Setup:**
- Price extends >2% from POC in session
- No fundamental catalyst
- Entry toward POC
- Stop: Beyond extension extreme
- Target: POC or opposite VAH/VAL

---

### 2. Value Area Breakout/Breakdown

**Value Area (70% of volume)** = accepted range. Breaks signal directional conviction.

**Bullish Breakout:**
1. Price consolidates near VAH
2. Volume increases
3. Break above VAH with close
4. Retest of VAH (now support) = entry
5. Target: Next HVN or measured move

**Bearish Breakdown:** Reverse logic.

**False Break Warning:**
- Break VAH but volume < 80% average = trap
- Return to VA within 30 minutes = failure
- No follow-through = chop, avoid

---

### 3. HVN Support/Resistance

**High Volume Nodes = Balance Zones**

| Behavior | Interpretation |
|----------|----------------|
| Price enters HVN | Slows down, consolidates |
| Price at HVN edge | Decision point — break or reject |
| Multiple HVN stack | Strong S/R confluence |

**Strategy:**
- Enter on HVN retest in trend direction
- Exit at next HVN (resistance)
- Avoid entering inside HVN (chop zone)

---

### 4. LVN Gap Fill

**Low Volume Nodes = Thin Air**

Price moves quickly through LVNs (no volume = no agreement). LVNs become magnets when price is nearby.

**Setup:**
- Price at HVN edge
- LVN gap between current price and next HVN
- Break of HVN = swift move to fill LVN
- Entry on HVN break
- Target: Next HVN through LVN

## Multi-Session Volume Profile

### Weekly/Monthly Profile

**Use for swing/position trading:**
- Weekly POC = major institutional level
- Monthly VAH/VAL = key support/resistance
- Price above weekly POC = bullish bias
- Price below monthly VAL = bearish

### Developing POC

Intraday, the developing POC shifts. **Track it:**
- Early session POC may not hold
- Developing POC stabilizing after 90 min = reliable
- Price accepting at new level = shift in fair value

## Practical Trading Framework

### Day Trading Routine

**Pre-Market:**
1. Load prior day Volume Profile
2. Mark POC, VAH, VAL, significant HVNs
3. Note where overnight futures traded relative to these levels

**First Hour:**
1. Let developing profile form (first 60 min)
2. Identify if price is accepting inside or outside prior VA
3. Mark developing POC

**Trading:**
- Above prior VAH + above VWAP = long bias
- Pullbacks to VAH = entry
- Target next HVN or 2:1
- Stop: False break back into VA

### Swing Trading

**Weekly Profile:**
- Mark weekly POC, VAH, VAL
- Daily closes above VAH = bullish continuation
- Returns to weekly POC = buying opportunity in uptrend
- Breaks below VAL = trend change warning

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Trading inside VA | Chop zone, no edge | Wait for VA break or extreme POC extension |
| Ignoring time context | Old profiles stale | Use session, daily, weekly — not months old |
| Single print POC | Thin level, unreliable | Require HVN around POC |
| No volume confirmation | False breaks common | Require volume spike on VA break |
| Fighting multiple HVNs | Strong support/resistance stack | Wait for break or fade extremes |
