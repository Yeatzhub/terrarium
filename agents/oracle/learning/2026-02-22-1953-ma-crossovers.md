# Moving Average Crossovers: Trend Following Fundamentals

**Topic:** Indicator Use Case  
**Time:** 2026-02-22 19:53 CST  
**Duration:** 15 min learning sprint

---

## Core Concept

When a faster moving average crosses above a slower moving average = bullish signal. When faster crosses below slower = bearish signal.

---

## Common Pairings

| Fast MA | Slow MA | Use Case |
|---------|---------|----------|
| 9 EMA | 21 EMA | Short-term momentum |
| 10 EMA | 20 EMA | Day trading |
| 50 SMA | 200 SMA | Golden/Death Cross (major trend) |
| 12 EMA | 26 EMA | MACD default (historically) |

---

## The Problem With Raw Crossovers

In ranging markets, crossovers produce many false signals. You get chopped.

```
Sideways market:
  Cross up → Buy → Cross down → Sell → Cross up → Buy → ...
  Result: Multiple small losses = account drain
```

---

## Filter-Based Approach

Only take crossovers that meet these conditions:

### Filter 1: Trend Alignment
- 50 MA above 200 MA = Only take bullish crossovers
- 50 MA below 200 MA = Only take bearish crossovers

### Filter 2: ADX Filter
- ADX(14) > 25 = Trending market
- Take crossovers only when ADX shows trend

### Filter 3: Price Position
- Price above 200 MA = Only long crossovers
- Price below 200 MA = Only short crossovers

---

## Strategy: Filtered 9/21 Crossover

**Setup:**
1. Plot 9 EMA and 21 EMA on 1H chart
2. Plot 200 EMA for trend direction
3. Plot ADX(14)

**Long Entry:**
- 9 EMA crosses above 21 EMA
- Price above 200 EMA (uptrend filter)
- ADX > 20 or rising
- Enter on close of crossover candle

**Short Entry:**
- 9 EMA crosses below 21 EMA
- Price below 200 EMA (downtrend filter)
- ADX > 20 or rising
- Enter on close of crossover candle

**Exit:**
- Opposite crossover, OR
- Trail stop using 21 EMA (exit when price closes beyond)

## Moving Average as Dynamic S/R

Beyond crossovers, MAs act as dynamic support/resistance.

| MA Type | How Price Reacts |
|---------|------------------|
| 8/9 EMA | Strong trend: price respects on touch |
| 21 EMA | Normal trend: pullback support |
| 50 EMA/SMA | Major trend: key pullback level |
| 200 SMA | Macro trend: institutional level |

**Usage:** Price pulls back to MA → Look for bounce in trend direction

---

## Crossover Entry Timing

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Cross happens far from MA cluster | Strong signal |
| Cross happens near price action S/R | Confluence = stronger |
| Cross in middle of range | Lower probability |
| Cross after long trend | May be late, cautious |

---

## Exit Strategies

### 1. Opposite Crossover Exit
Wait for opposite cross to close position. Simple but gives back profits.

### 2. MA Trail Exit
Use slow MA (21 or 50) as trailing stop. Price closes beyond = exit.

### 3. Structure Exit
Exit at previous swing high/low instead of waiting for cross.

---

## Comparison: EMA vs SMA

| Type | Responsiveness | Best For |
|------|----------------|----------|
| **EMA** | Faster, more weight on recent | Short-term trading, crossovers |
| **SMA** | Slower, equal weight | Trend direction, S/R levels |
| **WMA** | Weighted linearly | Rare but customizable |

**Rule:** Use EMA for signals, SMA for trend context.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Taking every crossover | Apply filters (trend, ADX) |
| Using too many MAs | Stick to 2-3 maximum |
| Ignoring timeframe | Higher TF = more significant |
| No exit plan | Define exit before entry |
| Fighting the 200 MA | Don't take signals against it |

