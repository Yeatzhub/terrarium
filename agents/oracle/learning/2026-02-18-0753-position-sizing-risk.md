# Position Sizing & The 1% Risk Rule
*Quick reference for immediate application*

## The Core Principle

**Never risk more than 1-2% of total account equity on a single trade.**

This isn't about position size — it's about *maximum loss* per trade.

---

## The Formula

```
Position Size = (Account Risk $) ÷ (Trade Risk $)
```

Where:
- **Account Risk** = Account Balance × Risk % (typically 0.01 for 1%)
- **Trade Risk** = Entry Price − Stop Loss Price (absolute $ risk per share/unit)

---

## Concrete Example

**Account:** $50,000  
**Risk Tolerance:** 1% = $500 max loss per trade  
**Setup:** Long AAPL at $185, stop at $180

```
Trade Risk per share = $185 − $180 = $5
Position Size = $500 ÷ $5 = 100 shares
Total Capital Deployed = 100 × $185 = $18,500 (37% of account)
Maximum Loss = $500 (1% of account)
```

---

## Why This Matters

| Consecutive Losses | 10% Risk/Trade | 1% Risk/Trade |
|-------------------|----------------|---------------|
| 5 losses          | −40.9% account | −4.9% account |
| 10 losses         | −65.1% account | −9.6% account |
| 20 losses         | −87.8% account | −18.2% account |

**Survival > Growth.** A 50% drawdown requires a 100% gain just to break even.

---

## Quick Mental Math (Intraday)

**For stocks:**
- $10k account, 1% risk = $100 max loss
- $0.50 stop loss → 200 shares max
- $1.00 stop loss → 100 shares max
- $2.00 stop loss → 50 shares max

**Always calculate position size BEFORE entry.**

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Fixed share counts** — Buying 100 shares regardless of stop distance
2. **Dollar-amount anchoring** — "I'll risk $500" without checking account %
3. **Widening stops to justify size** — Moving stops to accommodate greed
4. **Ignoring correlation** — Five 1% positions in tech = ~5% sector risk

---

## Edge Case: Small Accounts

If 1% risk produces insufficient position size for your strategy:
- **Option A:** Widen stops (accept worse R:R)
- **Option B:** Trade lower-priced instruments
- **Option C:** Temporarily accept 2% risk, but cap daily loss at 3-4%

Never exceed 2% single-trade risk regardless of account size.

---

## One-Line Rule

> *Set stop first, calculate size second, enter third — never reverse this order.*
