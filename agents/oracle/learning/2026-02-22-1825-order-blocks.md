## What Is an Order Block?

An order block (OB) is the last opposing candle before a strong, impulsive move. It represents where institutional orders were placed.

---

## Core Concept

Institutions can't enter all at once—they leave footprints. The candle before the explosive move shows where they positioned.

```
Bullish Order Block:
- Last DOWN candle before strong up move
- Price often returns to it before continuing

Bearish Order Block:
- Last UP candle before strong down move
- Price often returns to it before continuing
```

---

## Identifying Order Blocks

### Bullish OB Checklist:
1. Find strong bullish impulse (break of structure)
2. Look back to last DOWN candle before the move
3. Mark the high and low of that candle
4. Price should return to retest this zone

### Bearish OB Checklist:
1. Find strong bearish impulse (break of structure)
2. Look back to last UP candle before the move
3. Mark the high and low of that candle
4. Price should return to retest this zone

## Order Block Quality Filters

| Quality Factor | Strong OB | Weak OB |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| **Impulse** | Strong (3+ candles) | Weak (1-2 candles) |
| **Volume** | High on impulse | Low on impulse |
| **Time** | Recent (same session) | Old (> session ago) |
| **Retest** | Fresh (not yet touched) | Mitigated (already tested) |
| **Alignment** | With higher TF trend | Against trend |

---

## Entry Strategy

### Standard OB Entry:
1. Identify OB on 15m or 1H
2. Wait for price to return to OB zone
3. Look for rejection candle (engulfing, pin bar)
4. Enter on candle close
5. Stop on other side of OB
6. Target: Next liquidity pool (swing high/low)

### Refined Entry:
1. Identify OB on 1H
2. Drop to 5m or 15m for entry
3. Wait for smaller OB within larger OB
4. Enter on smaller timeframe confirmation
5. Tighter stop, better R:R

## Order Block vs Fair Value Gap

| Concept | What It Is | How to Trade |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| **Order Block** | Candle before impulse | Entry zone |
| **Fair Value Gap (FVG)** | Imbalance between candles | Entry/target zone |
| **Combined** | OB + FVG overlap | Highest probability zone |

**Best entries:** OB that overlaps with FVG = confluence zone

---

## Mitigation vs Invalidation

| Status | Meaning | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Fresh OB** | Not yet tested by price | High probability entry zone |
| **Mitigated OB** | Price has touched and reacted | Lower probability, may skip |
| **Invalidated OB** | Price broke through with close | OB failed, look for next one |

---

## Timeframe Hierarchy

| TF | Role | OB Significance |
|----|------|-----------------|
| 4H+ | Direction | Major OB, strong levels |
| 1H | Primary OB | Standard entries |
| 15m | Entry timing | Refined entries |
| 5m | Precision | Micro entries |

**Rule:** Higher TF OB = more reliable. Use lower TF for entry precision.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|------------|
| Marking every candle as OB | Look for impulsive moves FIRST, then find OB |
| Entering without confirmation | Wait for rejection candle at OB |
| Ignoring mitigation | Fresh OBs work best |
| Wrong timeframe context | Check higher TF for bias |
| No stop loss | Place stop beyond OB |
