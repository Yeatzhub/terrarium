# Volume Spread Analysis (VSA): Reading Institutional Footprints

**Topic:** Indicator Use Case  
**Time:** 2026-02-22 15:24 CST  
**Duration:** 15 min learning sprint

---

## Core Principle

Volume reveals institutional activity. Wide spreads + high volume = strength. Narrow spreads + high volume = weakness.

---

## The Three Components

| Component | What It Shows |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Spread** | Range of the candle (high - low) |
| **Volume** | How much traded in that period |
| **Close** | Where price closed within the spread |

---

## Key VSA Signals

### 1. Stopping Volume (Bottom Reversal)

**Appearance:**
- Wide spread down candle
- High volume
- Close in middle or upper third

**Meaning:** Smart money absorbing selling pressure. Demand entering.

**Action:** Look for long setup on next candle.

---

### 2. No Demand (Bearish)

**Appearance:**
- Narrow spread up candle
- Low volume
- Close near middle

**Meaning:** No institutional buying. Rally is weak.

**Action:** Look for short entry on weakness.

---

### 3. No Supply (Bullish)

**Appearance:**
- Narrow spread down candle
- Low volume
- Close near middle

**Meaning:** No institutional selling. Pullback is weak.

**Action:** Look for long entry on strength.

---

### 4. Climax Volume (Exhaustion)

**Appearance:**
- Extremely high volume
- Wide spread
- Close near middle or opposite extreme

**Meaning:** Exhaustion. Last of buyers/sellers entering.

**Action:** Prepare for reversal. Wait for confirmation.

---

### 5. Effort vs Result Divergence

**Pattern:** High volume effort + small price movement = potential reversal

**Example:**
- High volume pushing up
- Price barely moves (narrow spread)
- Result: Selling pressure absorbing buying

**Action:** Fade the move.

---

## Effort-Result Matrix

| Volume | Spread | Close | Signal |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| High | Wide | Near top | Strong up move |
| High | Wide | Near bottom | Strong down move |
| High | Wide | Middle | Stopping volume |
| High | Narrow | Middle | Effort-Result divergence (reversal) |
| Low | Narrow | Middle | No demand/supply (continuation) |

---

## Practical Application

### Step 1: Identify Trend
- Higher highs/lows = uptrend
- Lower highs/lows = downtrend

### Step 2: Find Key Level
- Previous swing high/low
- Support/resistance
- VWAP

### Step 3: Wait for VSA Signal at Level

**For Long:**
- Price at support
- Stopping volume or No Supply candle
- Enter on next candle confirmation

**For Short:**
- Price at resistance
- Climax volume or No Demand candle
- Enter on next candle confirmation

---

## Volume Zones

| Zone | Volume Level | Interpretation |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Very high** | > 2× average | Climax, reversal likely |
| **High** | > 1.5× average | Active participation |
| **Average** | 0.8×–1.2× | Normal activity |
| **Low** | < 0.5× average | Lack of interest, continuation |

---

## Combined With Price Action

| VSA Signal | Price Action | Entry Trigger |
|------------|--------------|---------------|
| No Supply | At support | Bullish engulfing |
| No Demand | At resistance | Bearish engulfing |
| Stopping Volume | After downtrend | Higher low forms |
| Climax Volume | After extended run | MSB in opposite direction |

## Quick Reference

```
Wide spread + High volume + Close middle = Stopping volume (reversal)
Narrow spread + Low volume = No demand/supply (continuation)
High volume + Little movement = Effort-Result divergence (reversal)
Very high volume at extremes = Climax (exhaustion)
```

> "Volume is the fuel. Spread shows the result. Close reveals who's in control."
