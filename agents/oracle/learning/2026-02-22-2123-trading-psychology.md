# Trading Psychology: FOMO and Revenge Trading

## The Psychology Problem

Most traders fail not from lack of knowledge, but from emotional execution. Two killers dominate: FOMO (entering late) and Revenge Trading (overtrading after loss).

---

## FOMO: Fear of Missing Out

### What It Looks Like

| Symptom | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Chasing entries | Entering after move already extended |
| Ignoring setup rules | Taking trades that don't meet criteria |
| No stop loss | "This one will keep going" |
| Checking charts constantly | Anxiety about missing moves |

### The Root Cause

FOMO comes from:
- Seeing others profit (social media, chat rooms)
- Not having a defined plan
- Greed overriding discipline
- Recency bias (recent winners = fear of missing next one)

### The FOMO Test

Before entering, ask:

| Question | If YES | If NO |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Is this my setup? | Proceed to checks | NO TRADE |
| Is the entry at my level? | Proceed | NO TRADE |
| Has the move already extended? | Wait for pullback | Consider entry |
| Am I entering because of FOMO or plan? | Stop. Wait. | Proceed |

## Revenge Trading: The Loss Spiral

### What It Looks Like

| Symptom | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Immediate re-entry | Taking another trade right after loss |
| Increasing size | "I need to make it back" |
| Abandoning rules | "The system isn't working" |
| Emotional escalation | Anger, frustration, desperation |

### The Math of Revenge

```
Starting account: $10,000
Loss 1: -$500 (5%) → Revenge mode
Loss 2: -$600 (6% of remaining) → Desperation
Loss 3: -$800 (9% of remaining) → Tilt
Loss 4: -$1,100 → Account down 30% in one session

Recovery needed: 43% gain to break even
```

### The Recovery Trap

After a big loss, traders think: "I need one big win to recover."

Reality: Recovering from loss requires larger % gain than the loss.

| Loss | Gain Needed to Recover |
|------|----------------------|
| 10% | 11% |
| 20% | 25% |
| 30% | 43% |
| 50% | 100% |
| 70% | 233% |

## Prevention Protocols

### FOMO Prevention

1. **The 5-Minute Pause**
   - When you feel urgency, wait 5 minutes
   - Most "sure things" will still be there (or not)

2. **Plan Before Session**
   - Define setups before markets open
   - You either have a plan, or you become the plan

3. **Daily Opportunity Limit**
   - Set max trades per day (e.g., 3)
   - Forces selectivity, reduces FOMO

4. **Social Media Blackout**
   - Don't look at others' trades during session
   - Your setups, your timeline, your rules

### Revenge Trading Prevention

1. **Post-Loss Rule**
   - After ANY loss: 15-minute mandatory break
   - Step away from screens completely

2. **Daily Loss Limit**
   - Set max daily loss (e.g., 2% or 3 trades)
   - Hit it = session over, no exceptions

3. **Size Freeze**
   - After loss, position size stays the same
   - NEVER increase size to "make it back"

4. **The "Why" Check**
   - Before next trade after loss, write why you're taking it
   - If reason includes "recover," DON'T TAKE IT

## Mental Frameworks

### The Casino Mindset

Every trade is independent. Previous outcomes don't affect next trade's probability. The market doesn't "owe" you anything.

### The Quarterback Mindset

After throwing an interception, elite QBs forget it immediately—the next drive is what matters. Same with trading: the next trade is independent.

### The Employee Mindset

Treat trading like a job. You don't get emotional about a spreadsheet error—you fix it and move on. Trading is probability management, not personal validation.

---

## Recovery Protocol (When Already in Tilt)

1. **Stop trading immediately**
2. **Close all positions** if unable to think clearly
3. **Physical reset**: Walk, exercise, shower
4. **Review**: What triggered the spiral?
5. **Minimum 24-hour break** before next session
6. **Return smaller**: Cut position size in half for first day back
