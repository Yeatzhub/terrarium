# eBay Photo Setup Checklist

Good photos = more views = higher prices = faster sales.

---

## 🌅 Lighting Setup

### Best: Natural Light
- [ ] Near large window during day
- [ ] Overcast or indirect sun (no harsh shadows)
- [ ] Turn off indoor lights (color temp mismatch)

### Alternative: Artificial Light
- [ ] Two light sources at 45° angles
- [ ] Daylight bulbs (5000K) if possible
- [ ] Diffuse with white sheet/curtain if too harsh

---

## 🖼️ Background

- [ ] Plain white/neutral background (bed sheet, poster board)
- [ ] Smooth surface (table, floor with cloth)
- [ ] No clutter or distractions
- [ ] Contrasting color to item (dark item → light bg, vice versa)

---

## 📷 Required Shots

For every item, take:

1. [ ] **Full item** — entire object in frame, straight-on
2. [ ] **Angle view** — 3/4 angle to show depth
3. [ ] **Detail shot** — model numbers, labels, branding
4. [ ] **Any flaws** — scratches, wear, damage (be honest!)
5. [ ] **Scale reference** — ruler/coin/hand for size context
6. [ ] **Accessories** — cables, remotes, boxes included

**Multiple items?** Show everything together, then individual shots.

---

## 📱 Camera Settings

- [ ] **Resolution:** Highest available
- [ ] **Flash:** OFF (causes glare/harsh shadows)
- [ ] **Focus:** Tap to focus before shooting
- [ ] **Steady:** Use both hands, or prop phone on books/box

---

## 💡 Pro Tips

- **Fill the frame** — item should be 80% of image
- **Eye level** — shoot straight at item, not from above
- **Clean first** — wipe dust, fingerprints off
- **Multiple pics** — eBay allows 12 free photos, use them!
- **Vertical items** → vertical photo
- **Horizontal items** → horizontal photo

---

## 🚀 Quick Start (10 min setup)

1. Find window with good light
2. Lay white sheet on table/floor
3. Place item on sheet
4. Turn off room lights
5. Open window coverings
6. Take 6 photos per item
7. Review on computer, pick best 8-12
